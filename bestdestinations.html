Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, formerly known as Ceylon, and officially the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, is an island country in South Asia. It lies in the Indian Ocean, southwest of the Bay of Bengal. The wide combination of British colonial heritage, beautiful landscapes and friendly Sri Lankans make it a charming destination. The tropical island has been spotted by travelers in recent years since the end of the 26-year civil war in 2009.

Zakynthos

Zakynthos or Zante meaning "amazing and loving" is a Greek island. It is the third largest and deservedly one of the most popular islands in the Ionian Sea - its idyllic beauty and abundance of natural wonders do not leave anyone indifferent. It is located south of Kefalonia and is world famous for its beautiful beaches and intense nightlife, which makes it a favorite destination for young people. There are countless reasons to visit Zakynthos.

Egypt

With its incredible history, which stretches back in time, until the birth of civilizations, Egypt is considered one of the oldest tourist destinations on Earth. For thousands of years, awe-inspiring temples and pyramids have made a very strong impression on tourists. Although most visitors come for the ancient monuments, there are some that are attracted by natural attractions: the Red Sea coast is famous for coral reefs and seaside resorts, and a trip across the Sahara will take the visitor to refreshing freshwater oases.

The Dominican Republic

The Dominican Republic is a country located on the island of Hispaniola in the Greater Antilles archipelago of the Caribbean region. Tourism is one of the fueling factors in the Dominican Republic's economic growth. The Dominican Republic is the most popular tourist destination in the Caribbean. With the construction of projects like Cap Cana, San Souci Port in Santo Domingo, Casa De Campo and the Hard Rock Hotel & Casino (ancient Moon Palace Resort) in Punta Cana, the Dominican Republic expects increased tourism activity in the upcoming years.

Malta

Malta, officially known as the Republic of Malta and formerly Melita, is a Southern European island country consisting of an archipelago in the Mediterranean Sea and is close to both neighboring Sicily and Africa. Malta has an almost mythical quality to it that comes from its eerie underwater caves, stunning temples, and medieval architecture. The streets here wind around towns and villages taking you past churches, markets, and museums, and some of the oldest buildings in the world can be found on this pretty isle.

Turkey

A country with a rich history and culture, spread over two continents, of exceptional geostrategic importance, Turkey is often called the “bridge between Europe and Asia”. Situated on the ruins of 4 empires, it offers countless sights and exciting destinations. This country is not only unusually beautiful, but also interesting in terms of architectural monuments and historical sites. Turkey is a country that definitely has a soul and will be able to captivate even the most demanding tourist.
