Sofia

The city of Sofia is the capital of Bulgaria. It is located in southwestern Bulgaria, in the Sofia plain, and also occupies parts near the northern slope of Vitosha Mountain. The capital Sofia offers various opportunities for entertainment - themed restaurants, bars, discos, dance clubs and more. In recent years, more and more hotels in Sofia are being renovated and enrich the vision of the city.The Bulgarian capital is one of the most ancient European capitals, it is believed that its history dates back to 8,000 years BC.

Rome

Rome, or the Eternal City, has attracted visitors for over 2,000 years. It is one of the most majestic and romantic cities in the world. Rome has an attractive mix of spectacular sights and bustling life. Shopping by Italian designers, tempting ice cream, sparkling cappuccino and fine wines are just some of the things you can try. The historic city center is part of the UNESCO cultural heritage with its beautiful palaces, ancient Roman ruins, exquisite statues and basilicas of thousands of years.

Budapest

Budapest is the capital of Hungary. It consists of two historic cities located on both banks of the Danube - Buda and Pest, which merged in 1873. The city will impress you with a rich architectural and historical heritage, majestic nature and an incomparable combination of culture, fine cuisine and thermal baths. In Budapest there are architectural monuments such as the impressive complex of the Hungarian Parliament, the Fisherman's Bastion, the Royal Palace, the Church of St. Matthias, the Cathedral of St. Stephen, the National Opera and many others.
